Get Help Now

If you are in immediate danger, feel unable to stay safe, or think you may act on suicidal thoughts, use the emergency options below right now.

Emergency: act now

If you may hurt yourself, have already taken action, have a weapon nearby, or cannot stay safe, do not wait for a message, email, or callback.

  • Call 119 for ambulance / fire emergency.
  • Call 110 for police emergency.
  • Go to the nearest hospital or emergency room now.
  • Tell someone near you immediately that you are not safe to be alone.

If you are not in immediate danger but you are struggling

You still deserve help now. Do these next:

  1. Move away from anything you could use to hurt yourself.
  2. Go where another person is, or ask someone to stay with you.
  3. Call one of the numbers above.
  4. Tell one person clearly: “I am not safe alone right now.”
  5. Do not isolate, drink heavily, or stay alone with suicidal thoughts.

For friends or family nearby

If you are with someone who may be suicidal:

  • Stay with them.
  • Remove immediate dangers if you can do so safely.
  • Call 119 or 110 if the risk is immediate.
  • Take them to a hospital if needed.
  • Use the Help Someone page for more guidance.

About Nailscar Ministry

Nailscar Ministry is a faith-based suicide prevention, survivor support, awareness, and crisis-direction ministry.

Important: We are not medical providers, psychiatrists, psychologists, therapists, or emergency services. We do not replace crisis lines, hospitals, or licensed mental health care.

Our role is to point people toward the right resources, offer pastoral care, and speak honestly and compassionately about suicide, grief, hope, and survival.

English

If you are in danger, get help now.

Call 119 for ambulance, 110 for police, or go to the nearest hospital. If you are overwhelmed but not alone, call a crisis support line and tell someone clearly that you are not safe.
